Mobile phone Overview

The history of the cell phone dates back to 1973, when Motorola engineer Martin Cooper made the first-ever cell phone call on the DynaTAC 8000X12. The prototype he used was bulky and heavy, weighing 2.4 lb (1.1 kg)2. In the following decades, cell phones became smaller, lighter, and more advanced, with features such as flip design, GSM network, and mass production1. Samsung, Nokia, Siemens, and Motorola were some of the leading manufacturers of cell phones in the late 20th century1
